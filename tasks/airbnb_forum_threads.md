# Airbnb Community Forum - thread playbook

Forum: https://community.withairbnb.com/
Target sub-communities:
- Hosting → Hosting discussion
- Hosting → Hosting tips
- Help → Ask about your listing
- Community Center → Let's talk about you

## Rules of engagement
- Post from your real host profile (you have one)
- Never open with a link. Tell a story, share the problem, and drop the link
  in a reply ONLY when another user asks "what tool?"
- Max 1 new thread per day. Reply to 5 other threads per day to stay
  top-of-mind without spamming.
- The moderators flag anything that looks like promotion. Our angle is always
  "fellow host sharing what worked for me." The tool is never the hero.
- You already have 2 active threads running. The 8 drafts below are NEW,
  different angles so you don't get flagged for duplicate content.

**Title:** How I cut repetitive guest messages by ~70% (sharing what worked)

**Body:**
> Hey all. Wanted to share something that actually moved the needle for me this
> summer.
>
> I host a 2BR in Geneva and was getting 5-10 of the same questions every
> week from guests: nearest grocery store, best coffee, how to get to the
> lake, is tap water safe, do I tip taxis. I had a PDF welcome book already.

> 1. Everything is within 10 minutes walk of the flat (not "top 50 things to
>    do in Geneva")
> 2. Every place has walking time, not just address
> 3. One page per category, max
>
> Message volume dropped hard. Guests started mentioning the guide in reviews.
>
> Two questions for the community:
> - What do YOU put in your welcome book that guests actually read?
> - How do you keep it updated when a place closes or a new one opens?

## Thread 3 - "Tested my welcome book on 5 different guests"

**Title:** I A/B tested my welcome book on 5 guests. Here's what they read and ignored.

**Body:**
> I got curious what guests actually use in my welcome book vs. what they
> ignore, so I asked five of them directly (in exchange for a small discount
> on checkout). Here's what came back:
>
> Read:
> - Walking distances to transit, groceries, and cafes (every single one)
> - "If the heating doesn't work, do this" emergency section
> - Local taxi / ride app (Uber vs. Bolt here)
>
> Ignored:
> - "About your host" paragraph
> - Generic city history
> - The 20-item bucket list of tourist attractions
> - House rules (they assumed these were in the Airbnb listing already)
>
> Worth cutting. Now my welcome book is 4 pages instead of 12. Reviews
> improved.
>
> Anyone else audited theirs recently? What did you cut?

## Thread 5 - "Superhost onboarding tip for new hosts"

**Title:** What I wish I'd known before my first review (for new hosts)

**Body:**
> New-host thread. If you're about to receive your first 5 bookings, here's
> what moved my rating from 4.6 to 4.9:
>
> 1. The welcome book is more important than the check-in message. Guests
>    skim the check-in. They keep the welcome book open all week.
> 2. Include walking times to everything. Addresses are useless - "7 min
>    walk" is gold.
> 3. Three closest restaurants, three closest cafes, three closest groceries.
>    Don't list 20 of each. Decision fatigue is real.
> 4. Write the "what to do if X" section for the top 5 things that go wrong:
>    wifi down, heating cold, washing machine locked, lockbox stuck, trash
>    day.
> 5. Put the emergency numbers of the actual country, not 911. Saved me
>    twice.
>
> What else would you tell a brand-new host?
